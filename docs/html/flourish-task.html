UK trade

This is the story I have created based on the data on UK imports and exports.

The UK's trade with other countries has more than doubled since 1999.

It went up from 500 million in 1999 to over 1 billion in 2016.

Exports from the UK to other countries around the world are one part of the UK's trade.

They have increased from £241,472 million in 1999 to £528,034 million in 2016.

Imports from other countries have developed similarly and increased since 1999.

Every year since 1999, the UK is importing more than it is exporting.

This resulted in a trade deficit of £55,746 million in 2016.

However, out of all 189 countries the UK has traded with in 2016, it had a trade surplus with the majority of them.

One of them are the United States of America — the UK's biggest trading partner.

In 2016, the UK has had a £33,256 million trade surplus with the United States of America.

In Europe, the UK has a trade deficit with most countries.

The UK has the largest trade deficit with Germany. In 2016, it had a deficit of £25,995 million.
